Crops not Watered, Fruit Rotting: Kandahar’s agriculture hit by war, drought and closed customs gates

Farmers across Afghanistan have been hit hard by drought and fighting in recent years. In this report, we look at how this has affected agriculture in one province, Kandahar, famous for its fruit production. Much of the fruit crop is – or should be – exported, yet the frequent and unpredictable closure of crossings into Pakistan has been an additional headache for farmers and exporters in the province. Those trying to dry and process food crops in Kandahar’s industrial park have also been hit by its collapse since the takeover of Afghanistan by the Taleban. AAN’s Ali Mohammad Sabawoon has been hearing from farmers and others about the woes affecting Kandahar’s agricultural economy.

technology solutions for droughts agricultural

Absolutely, droughts are a critical consequence of climate change, impacting various regions globally. Technological solutions can significantly contribute to mitigating the effects of drought. Here are some potential technology solutions specifically targeted at addressing drought caused by climate change:

  1. . Precision Agriculture Technology: Use of Drones and Sensors Drones for Crop Monitoring: Drones equipped with various sensors can monitor soil moisture levels, crop health, and detect areas susceptible to drought stress. They aid in efficient water usage and crop management. Soil Moisture Sensors: These devices help farmers to precisely measure soil moisture content, allowing for more accurate irrigation, reducing water waste, and optimizing crop growth.
  2. Desalination Technologies: Solar-Powered Desalination Plants Solar-Powered Desalination: Utilizing renewable energy sources such as solar power to operate desalination plants can provide a sustainable solution to water scarcity in drought-affected regions.
  3. Water Recycling and Reuse: Advanced Water Treatment Systems Greywater Recycling: Implementing systems that recycle greywater from households and industries for non-potable uses like irrigation or flushing, reducing the demand on freshwater resources. Advanced Water Purification Technologies: Implementing advanced water treatment systems and technologies can efficiently treat wastewater, making it safe for consumption or reuse.
  4. Climate Data Analytics and Prediction: Machine Learning for Drought Prediction Machine Learning Models: Using historical climate data and machine learning algorithms to predict drought patterns, helping in early warning systems and proactive drought management.
  5. Community Engagement and Education Platforms: Mobile Apps and Online Platforms Education and Awareness Apps: Developing mobile applications or online platforms that educate and encourage individuals to adopt water-saving practices, promoting behavioral change. Community Sharing Platforms: Platforms where communities can share information, strategies, and best practices for water conservation and drought resilience.
